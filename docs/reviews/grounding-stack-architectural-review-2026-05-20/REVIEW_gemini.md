# Engram Grounding Stack — Architectural Review by Gemini CLI

## Verdict
continue-with-revisions

## Risk Register

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Net Severity |
|----|------|------------|--------|--------------|
| R1 | Operator Toil Bottleneck | H | H | H |
| R2 | Zero-Percent Resolution Pipeline | H | H | H |
| R3 | Blind Degradation via Missing Eval Surface | H | H | H |
| R4 | Local-LLM Triage Complexity (RFC 0056) | M | M | M |
| R5 | Premature Network Dispatch of Noise | H | M | M |

**R1 Operator Toil Bottleneck:** The current requirement for per-grant human approval via `engram claim-grounding grants approve` strictly limits throughput. With hundreds of unknown entities generated by Phase 4 and a 0% resolution rate, operators will quickly abandon the process due to friction and low reward.

**R2 Zero-Percent Resolution Pipeline:** A 0% resolution rate highlights a critical impedance mismatch between Phase 4 outputs (noisy, ungroundable personal surfaces) and the constrained RFC 0053 network boundary (`search_query == surface_form`). The architecture is faithfully executing queries that are structurally doomed to fail or return ambiguous results.

**R3 Blind Degradation via Missing Eval Surface:** There is no systemic tracking of resolution rates, error budgets, or labeled sets. Without these metrics, the impact of architectural changes (like RFC 0056) or upstream Phase 4 prompt tweaks cannot be empirically validated, risking silent regressions.

**R4 Local-LLM Triage Complexity (RFC 0056):** Inserting a local LLM into the `entity_grounding_triage_actions` flow introduces significant latency, schema churn, and prompt versioning burdens. While it aims to reduce toil, it swaps manual review for operational fragility and false-suppression risks.

**R5 Premature Network Dispatch of Noise:** Dispatching inherently noisy or personal artifact surfaces to the external broker wastes network resources and risks indirect leakage of local context through search query patterns, even if the strict RFC 0053 boundary is maintained.

## Recommendations (Ranked)

**1. Shift to Confidence-Thresholded Auto-Approval with Post-Hoc Audit**
- **Action:** Replace the per-grant manual approval gate with a confidence-based auto-approval mechanism. Grants that meet strict heuristic criteria (e.g., proper noun capitalization, length constraints, term frequency in corpus) transition immediately to `approved` state. Divert low-confidence or randomly sampled grants to the existing manual review queue (`claim_grounding_grants` review surface) to maintain an audit trail.
- **Why now:** Directly attacks R1. Operator toil must be decoupled from throughput to scale the grounding stack.
- **Tradeoff:** Increases the risk of dispatching low-value queries to the external broker if heuristics are loose, costing network time and potential token spend (if applicable).
- **Sequencing:** Can land immediately using deterministic rules before any LLM-based triage is introduced.

**2. Push Disambiguation and Noise Reduction Upstream to Phase 4**
- **Action:** Modify Phase 4 entity extraction to emit a "groundability score" or categorize entities explicitly as "personal", "local", or "public". Only entities flagged as "public" with high confidence should enter the drafting phase (`engram entity-grounding draft`).
- **Why now:** Solves R2 at the root. The materializer cannot fix garbage-in. Reducing the volume of ungroundable entities entering the pipeline is more efficient than filtering them out via triage or human review.
- **Tradeoff:** Requires modifying Phase 4 prompts and schemas, potentially impacting upstream extraction pipelines and adding latency to Phase 4.
- **Sequencing:** Should be prototyped against a representative benchmark set before modifying the main Phase 4 pipeline.

**3. Establish Formal End-to-End Evaluation Surface**
- **Action:** Create a labeled evaluation dataset consisting of known groundable, ambiguous, and ungroundable entities. Implement a telemetry script (local only) to track the resolution rate (`status='resolved'` vs `ambiguous` vs `failed` in `claim_grounding_responses`) across the pipeline stages.
- **Why now:** Mitigates R3. You cannot optimize a 0% resolution rate if you cannot measure incremental improvements.
- **Tradeoff:** Requires upfront engineering effort to build the harness and manual effort to curate the initial labeled set.
- **Sequencing:** Must be completed before evaluating the effectiveness of either Phase 4 upstream changes or RFC 0056.

**4. Redesign Triage (RFC 0056) as Deterministic Pre-Filters Before LLM**
- **Action:** Revise RFC 0056 to rely heavily on fast, deterministic local rules (e.g., regex, stop-word lists, existing local index lookups) *before* invoking a local LLM. The LLM should only be used as a tie-breaker for edge cases, not as the primary filter.
- **Why now:** Reduces the complexity and latency risks (R4) of the proposed triage step while still providing a mechanism to suppress noisy grants.
- **Tradeoff:** Deterministic rules require maintenance and may not capture nuanced ungroundable entities as effectively as an LLM.
- **Sequencing:** Requires suspending RFC 0056 in its current form until deterministic filters are exhausted.

## Anti-Recommendations

- **Do not relax the RFC 0053 byte-exact rule (`search_query == surface_form`).** While it causes ambiguity, it is the fundamental privacy and trust boundary. Relaxing it to allow LLM-generated search queries risks leaking local claim context to the external provider.
- **Do not merge RFC 0056 as a pure LLM classifier.** The operational overhead and latency of a local LLM in the critical path for every extracted entity will compound the pipeline bottleneck, not relieve it.
- **Do not build complex post-materialization LLM disambiguation yet.** Before trying to use an LLM to pick the right entity from 5 materialized candidates, ensure the pipeline can actually fetch the right candidates. Fix the upstream noise first.

## 3-Month Plan

- **Month 1: Metrics & Upstream Signal**
  - Deliverable: Grounding evaluation harness with a curated 100-entity labeled set.
  - Deliverable: Phase 4 modifications to categorize "public" vs "local/personal" entities.
  - Metric: Pipeline intake volume reduced by 50% (filtering out personal surfaces).
  - Gate: Review of benchmark results against the new eval harness.
- **Month 2: Automated Throughput**
  - Deliverable: Heuristic-based auto-approval mechanism for high-confidence grants.
  - Deliverable: Post-hoc audit CLI (`engram claim-grounding grants audit-sample`).
  - Metric: Operator manual review time reduced by 90%; network dispatch volume monitored for noise.
  - Gate: Review of auto-approved dispatches to ensure external privacy boundary is uncompromised.
- **Month 3: Triage Refinement**
  - Deliverable: Deterministic pre-filter pipeline (revised RFC 0056) deployed before draft generation.
  - Deliverable: Local LLM tie-breaker implemented *only* for the ambiguous middle-tier of entities.
  - Metric: End-to-end resolution rate exceeds 20% on the benchmark set.
  - Gate: Architectural review of the local LLM performance and latency impact.

## Open Architectural Questions

- How do we fundamentally resolve ambiguous public entities (e.g., "Org X") when the RFC 0053 byte-exact rule prevents adding disambiguating context to the search query? Must this forever remain a manual operator task, or can we bring the candidates locally and disambiguate post-materialization?
- What is the acceptable false-positive rate for auto-approved grants reaching the external network? Are we optimizing for zero leakage of personal terms, or is there a tolerated threshold for the sake of automation?
- If Phase 4 upstream filtering is highly successful, does the complex triage step proposed in RFC 0056 become unnecessary architecture?
